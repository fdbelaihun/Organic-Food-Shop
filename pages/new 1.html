Land Preparation

Our organic foods are cultivated with all natural inputs and resources. Our farmers use all natural compost, and Fertilizer.

One of the most food contaminant on cultivation land is industrial wast products and synthetic residuals. The land where our farmers choose to cultivation is not not exposed for industrial wast products.

Cultivation

To minimize the risk of toxic substance contamination, our farmers use pesticides which is produced from organic materials with no artificial additives. They use non-contaminated water as well. They also use mechanical techniques to remove weeds.

Harvesting

Our farmers harvest their organic fruits, vegetables and crops at right maturity stage to maintain the moisture level and freshness of the food. The level of food maturity at which farmers harvested is an important factor for the quality of all farm food varieties.

Labeling

After the foods are harvested they immediately weighed, labeled and transported to the temporary storage rooms or delivered to our shops.

Transportation

To protect the Nation's plant and natural resources harmful plant pests, diseases, and curious weeds, USDA has the authority to prohibit or restrict the interstate movement of certain plants and plant products, including fruits and vegetables. We transport our whole fresh organic food to our shop very cautiously to keep it fresh and prevent contamination.

Stocking

From the start of cultivation until we received the foods to our shop, we monitor and evaluate the quality of each food. Once after we received the food, we stock it in the designated bins where it's ready for sale in the shop.
